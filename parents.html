Parent & Guardian Information

Schedule (sample)

  • 8:15a — Doors open, check‑in
  • 9:00a — Morning session begins
  • 12:00p — Lunch
  • 1:00p — Afternoon session begins
  • 4:00p — Dismissal & pick‑up

What to bring

  • Part A/B health form (if required), medications labeled
  • Blue card(s) signed by unit leader
  • Pencil/pen, notebook, water bottle
  • Lunch or lunch money (if sold onsite)
Can my Scout complete a badge in one day?

Many badges include prerequisites or requirements that can span multiple days or weeks. Counselors will document completed items and next steps on the blue card. If your Scout completes the prerequisites prior to attending the MBW or during a break and is signed off by their Counselor, it's possible for them to complete a merit badge that day.

How does check‑in work?

Follow signage to the registration desk. Please arrive 15–30 minutes early. Scouts should wear Class A uniform unless otherwise instructed.

What are the refund and transfer policies?

Policies are set per event on the event and will be posted on the registration page. Exceptions with be reviewed individually and as per the circumstances.

Who do I contact with questions?

Use our contact form and we’ll get back to you promptly.

Will lunch be provided?

This year, MBW will be providing lunch to volunteers and counselors only. This is how we are keeping the registration fee low. Please ensure your Scout has something for them to eat during the lunch break.

Can my Scout heat up their lunch from home?

Unfortunately, we cannot guarantee there will be a functional microwave or other option to heat a meal.

My Scout has severe food allergies.

Scouts with severe food allergies are strongly encouraged to sit in the designated area that will be free from airborne allergens.

Will there be food for sale?

We are planning to have a Trading Post. The Trading Post will have the typical snacks found at a Scouting America event. The MBW organizers will not have input regarding what will be for sale.

Are uniforms required to be worn at the MBW?

The MBW is an official NEIC event. All Scouts and Scouters are expected to wear their uniform for the duration of the day unless otherwise stated in the class catalog.

I don’t have a uniform because I’m an adult volunteer, committee member, and/or volunteer parent.

If you are a non-uniformed volunteer participating as Staff or on our Registration team, you don’t need to wear a uniform.

We appreciate your support in making these workshops successful.
